Key Forecast Insights
📉 Implied YoY Decline of −20.4%
Jan and Feb 2026 actuals came in at $16.6M and $22.0M respectively — both below their 2025 counterparts ($19.1M and $29.4M). This early-year pacing implies a full-year run-rate of $521.3M, down 20.4% from 2025's $654.8M. The seasonal pattern from 2025 is preserved in the forecast shape.
📅 Q4 Remains the Heaviest Quarter
Following the 2025 seasonal pattern, Q4 2026 is forecast at $224.3M — representing 43% of the full year. December alone is projected at $108.8M (20.9% of annual), consistent with the year-end spend acceleration observed in 2024 and 2025.
📊 Trend Forecast vs Sheet Pipeline: +$74M Gap
The trend-based forecast of $521.3M is $74M higher than the sheet's current pipeline data of $447.3M. This gap is most pronounced in Sep (+$44M), Dec (+$46M), and Oct (+$33M) — months where the sheet may have uncommitted or not-yet-entered deals that the trend model anticipates based on 2025 patterns.
⚠️ Jan–Feb Pacing Below 2025
January 2026 came in −13.0% below Jan 2025, and February came in −25.2% below Feb 2025. This slow start is the primary driver of the implied annual decline. If Mar–Jun actuals track closer to 2025 levels, the full-year outcome could be revised upward from the current $521M forecast.
